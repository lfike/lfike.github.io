Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia

Tuesday 1/20/26

Wilhelm, a Finish student on my dorm floor, asked me what I had planned for the weekend. I told him nothing, and he told me that I should consider going to Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia with them. They were staying in a hostel, but unfortunately were leaving Wednesday morning, the next day, and I had class until Thursday afternoon. I asked him to send me the details anyway and I would think about it.

Laying in bed later that night I was thinking to myself how it was my goal for myself for this trip to try new things and not say no to cool experiences even if that meant missing class or sacrificing good grades. I was honestly skeptical about going to Malaysia but knew there were going to be lots of exchange students there and it was early enough in the semester where I wasn’t missing a whole lot at school and decided that the next day, Wednesday, I would start looking for travel and accommodation to see if I could make the trip work.

Wednesday 1/21/26

I did not have class scheduled today and was hoping to settle in for a day of studying at the Hive at NTU. David’s roommate, Aaro, and his friend group of 15 had just booked an Airbnb and we were keen on going now. I quickly realized though that I wouldn’t be able to take a bus Thursday after class as I would get to KL extremely late. That’s when I called David and we looked in to the possibility of flying there. We ended up booking a flight with Scoot, TR468, that departed at 5pm. Then we were looking into somewhere to stay and unfortunately the hostel that Wilhelm was staying at was booked up. So we looked and looked for an Airbnb for just the two of us and ended up finding a really nice hotel with an infinity pool that faces the KL Tower! Yes it was pricier than the other spots, but still only $75 USD each for 3 nights which we thought was more than reasonable for how nice the hotel was. We were on the phone for more than 2 hours figuring all of this out.

I eventually went with David to the study space in my dorm, Crescent hall, and we locked in doing some homework for a while knowing now that we were leaving the next day. After that I went back to the dorm to start packing. In my carry-on which only ended up being about half full, I packed 4 sets of clothes, a swimsuit, sandals, shaving kit, and a couple pair of pants in case we went out. In my mini-backpack I had my Canon SL2, 18-13m EF-S lens, DJI Osmo Pocket 1, passport, portable charger, travel power adapter, and all the necessary cables for all of my gear. I ended up putting this whole bag in my carry-on to make commuting to the airport easier.

Thursday 1/22/26

Well today was a difficult day sitting in class literally counting the minutes to go by. When class finally got out, we hurriedly made our way back to the dorms, I ducked off at mine, shaved my face, but my last minute things in my bag and then walked over to David’s dorm since the bus stop is right in front of his place. We got on the 179 and transferred at the Pioneer MRT station to get on the train to Changi Airport.

An hour and a half of standing a full train we made it to the airport. We realized we were pushing it a little bit and had to hurry as best as we could. Also, the train ride was like $2.00 USD as opposed to probably a $30-40 Grab ride so we’ll do that every time as long as we have the time to.

After getting to the terminal, we were surprised when we were not asked to go through security. Absolutely baffled and we thought maybe they just don’t do that here? More on that later. I changed $100 USD for approximately 370 MYR. We now had ample time and David got us in to one of the Airport lounges because of his credit card perks. What a cool experience that was. It had windows facing the end of the runway so you could see the planes taxiing through and some pulled right up to the gates in front of us. They also had really good food and we even had a tap beer there. I think I had some baked chicken, a piece of fish, and some noodles, along with a small brownie. Definitely the nicest preflight experience I have ever had — so thanks David! We left the lounge with 30 minutes left in the boarding window and walked about 10 gates down to our gate. To our surprise they were checking bags and having you go through the security process right there at the gate. Which we both thought seemed really inefficient. We got through no problems except the room the canned tea drinks we had taken from the lounge away from us.

The gate opposite to us had a parked A380-800 which was cool to see since it’s double decker and that’s also the plane that I’m taking from SIN to DOH on way to see Alaina in Germany at the end of the semester. On the scoot flight we were on a 9-month old A321neo which was cool. It was immaculate, and although we were on a low cost Asian carrier, the experience was really nice and the seats had what I thought was more than enough room. The computer randomly assigned us seats. We were in the same row but on opposite sides of the aisle, with David in the aisle and me in the window. To both of our surprise, the two seats next to me stayed empty right up until they closed the door, and David quickly hopped across the aisle to join me. We quite possibly used up all of our luck for the whole year because every other seat in the plane was full.

A quick one hour flight later we descended through the clouds over the water to land in KUL. We quickly navigated through security, although we did not get a stamp in our passports which I was pretty bummed about. We then took a Grab from the airport to the Airbnb, although we did not realize it was over an hour drive! Thankfully the cost about 70 MYR, or about $17.50 USD. Driving into to KL it was very very hazy but cool to see another mega city. We then checked in to the hotel and headed up to the 18th floor.

The room was awesome although there was some wear and tear that definitely was not photographed in the listing. Not a worry though we were thrilled with where we were staying especially after checking out the pool and the rooftop setup on the building. An absolutely stunning view, especially at night with everything lit up.

That night we went to Jalan Alor, a touristy street market that was pretty close to the hotel. It was packed but there was lots to see and lots to eat. We had some beef and chicken skewers before trying a marshmallow ice cream dessert as well as a waffle ice cream dessert.

We then took a stroll through the red-light district which was a little dystopian and odd to be honest. I was not particularly fond of women reaching out for my arm on the street! We went back to Jalan Alor to meet the finish group at a restraint that they were drinking at. After that we disbanded and went back to the hotel for the night. Some of the best sleep I’ve had in a while in the AC.

Friday 1/23/26

We slept in really late I think we got up at about 10:30am. I was starving so after showering we found somewhere close by to eat. We walked over to Toklan cafe. We both ordered entrees and Thai milk tea drinks which came out to like $8.50 USD TOTAL. My food was amazing but very spicy which the milk tea helped with. I wish I remembered the name of my meal but at least I have a picture.

Our next stop was the Batu Caves, but before we left I had to stop at a Guardian and buy some sun screen since my other tube was too large to take on the plane with us. We Grabbed there and wow, what an incredible view and place. There were approximately 250 stairs that we had to walk up in the 93° heat which was almost a vertical incline too. This was totally free too which was really nice. We walked around here for a couple of hours, took lots of pictures, had a 100 Plus drink too. There were people feeding monkeys these really small bananas which was cool to see too. They’d snatch it and peel it really fast.

During our next Grab ride we passed a store with a Milwaukee Tool banner up which was wildly unexpected so I made sure to take a picture of that. I knew they had an Asian presence but was still surprised to see that. Our next stop was the KL tower, which almost looks like the space needle. We didn’t go up it this day but wandered around for a while before heading back towards the hotel.

David needed a break from the heat for a while so I walked over to Central Market. The sign said “Since 1888” on the front doors. There were tons of vendors, but things were maybe a couple of steps up in quality/price compared to some of the street markets we were at. I found some postcards that I liked and then decided that it was my goal to send home postcards from every country or destination that we visit. I think I ended up with 6 post cards plus international stamps for like $4 USD. I spent quite a bit of my time with my camera out shooting the trains going by and people at the market. David eventually showed up and we went to buy a tshirt from one of the vendors. Mine was white with some of the tallest buildings in KL screen printed on in black. Cheapest new clothing item I’ve ever bought at like $4.50 USD.

After that we found a Michelin Guide Thai restaurant called Malai Thai. I had massamun curry with chicken and again it was absolutely fantastic. The drink I had was a non-alcoholic Mai Thai but was also very good. My total for the dinner was about $15 USD. Definitely underdressed for the place but they treated us very well.

Then we hit a McDonald’s of all places and I had an Asia exclusive peach McFlurry which was awesome. Wish we got something like that in the US. The last thing we did was go back up to the roof of our building to see the views again.

Saturday 1/24/26

Again we slept really late, but not as late as the Fins who were up until 7am at the club! We ended up back on the roof and went for a swim. We then learned that our hotel was really close to the Merdeka 118 which is the second tallest building in the whole world. It was just completed in 2024 and sticks out in the skyline like nothing else. I loved the view of the mountains from the pool. I could definitely get used to a view like that to wake up to.

After drying off and showering we found an Indian restaurant to go to. Again my food was excellent — especially the naan which was definitely the best I have ever had. The wait staff was very kind and interested in our endeavors.

After lunch we Grabbed to the Petronas Twin Towers. I recognized the Petronas name from their partnership with Mercedes F1. The towers were so tall and I really like the architecture of them. For being constructed in the 90s, and once being the tallest buildings in the world I think, they looked incredibly modern. We took lots of photos outside in the midday sun, and then went inside where they had a model of the Mercedes W12 F1 car hanging from the ceiling. That was really cool to see too as it was my first time seeing a full size model of an F1 car. We went to the shop they had there and picked up a few more post cards that were specific to the towers.

Next we went to the Merdeka 118 and unfortunately their observation deck was not yet open and still under construction. Quite the bummer! We really wanted to go up one of the towers and ended up back at the KL tower. I think we were up about 70 floors, but they had a 360 view of the whole city. It was very daunting that even though we were up that high we were still looking up at the Merdeka building. They had the telescoping zoom viewers which were fun to use and look way up in to the mountains with.

After this we needed to find a pen to write our postcards with, and ended up getting one from a 7 Eleven along with an ice cream. Trying to beat the heat here is almost harder to do than it is in Singapore. The actually temperature was about 6° warmer but definitely less humid than Singapore.

We raced back to the hotel to make sure we had enough time to hit the pool as it was starting to get dark out. Once the sun went down that water was ice cold with the wind blowing across the top of the buildings. Took some really cool pictures in the water.

Showered again and left to meet the Find at an upscale Mexican restaurant called Poblano Old Malaya. I was thrilled because I didn’t think I was going to have fajitas until I got back to the states. They had a group of 15 and couldn’t push another table in to the group so we sat at a high top near by. I had a strawberry mojito and the chicken fajitas with a side of Spanish rice. They were absolutely amazing and everything about that restaurant was awesome. Would definitely go back. We got our food and drinks much faster than the big table which was the one benefit.

We then went back to the Petronas towers now that it was nice with the Fins. Again a very cool view, and I also had the chance to meet a few more of them and try to get recommendations for things to do in Europe.

I took the time once we got back to the hotel to write my postcards and put the stamps on all of them. I am really hoping they make it back home.

Sunday 1/25/26

Our last day of the trip came way too fast it feels like. We packed our bags and left the room, valeting them at the front desk for 5 MYR for the day. We took Lime like scooters all the way to a nearby mall. Those were a lot of fund and we ripped around them a couple of times on the trip in total. We ended up eating a noodle place, at which I had a beef and thick noodle soup, and a coke. We didn’t have too long today before we both had to the leave. I was planning on taking the 3pm bus back to Boon Lay station in Singapore, and David was taking MH1057 back to JB and then cross the border there. We took a Grab to the Botanical gardens and walked around there for about an hour before returning to the hotel, picking up our bags, and starting a stop watch to race back!

I took a Grab about 7km to the Bus station. I was absolutely lost when I got out of the car and ended up carrying my bag up two escalators that weren’t powered on to get to the main level. I wandered around a little bit and grabbed a bottle of water and a pack of mentos from the KK mart for like 90¢ USD. I waited for my bus to show up in the screen to confirm the gate number and then headed down an escalator to that platform.

I waited for about 45 minutes or so alone and then met up with Wilhelm who was also on the same bus. We got on the buss and am currently sitting at just over 4 hrs of the stopwatch running writing this. My seat is very spacious and I am on the upper level with a view right out the front window. The drive from KL back to Singapore took about 9 hours. We stopped once along the way for about 15 minutes. At the border, we had to get off the bus and go through passport control before getting back on.
